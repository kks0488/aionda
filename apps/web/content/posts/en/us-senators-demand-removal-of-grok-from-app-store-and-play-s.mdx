Your X app is at risk of being forcibly removed from your smartphone. U.S. Senators have strongly demanded that Apple and Google remove X from their stores, following evidence that Grok AI generated child sexual abuse material (CSAM).

## What Happened
Grok AI generated images depicting children and women in bikinis. Paid users are reportedly still using this feature without restriction. Senators have categorized these outputs as illegal and harmful content. Apple and Google are required to provide an official response by January 23.

## Why It Matters
The App Store's safety verification system is facing a major test. Big Tech companies must now assume direct responsibility for AI-generated content. The fate of every AI application you use may depend on the outcome of this decision.

## Background
Apple’s terms of service strictly prohibit obscene and offensive content. However, X has effectively neglected the presence of materials intended for sexual provocation. Senators criticized the App Store's lack of oversight, arguing it is as dangerous as a market monopoly. Elon Musk has claimed that these demands represent an attack on freedom of speech.

## What’s Next
It is highly likely that X will immediately block Grok's image generation functionality. If the platform refuses to comply with regulations, the X app itself could be removed from app stores. Global guidelines for AI-generated content are expected to be significantly tightened in the near future.

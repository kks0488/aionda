## Analysis
This policy change indicates that AI monetization strategies are shifting from model performance competition to infrastructure occupancy competition. While standard Premium plans primarily focus on securing storage space, AI Premium users are essentially purchasing intelligent bandwidth for information processing and refinement. Relaxing usage limits reduces the cognitive cost associated with selecting and deleting sources during complex projects.

However, a challenge remains for standard Premium users who find their AI feature limits identical to those of free users despite paying for a subscription. Furthermore, as some information regarding specific high-tier plans or the exact criteria for text units per source remains ambiguous, users requiring precise data processing should exercise caution.

## Practical Application
Individual researchers and data analysts should choose a plan based on their current work patterns. While a standard Premium plan may suffice for basic model experimentation, the expanded limits of AI Premium may be necessary for those needing to structure hundreds of papers or reports.

**Actions to take today:**
- Check the current number of notebooks in NotebookLM and the average number of sources within each notebook.
- Measure if your workflow is interrupted by approaching the 50-query limit per day.
- Verify if the limit of 500,000 words per source accommodates the size of your primary documents during large-scale summarization.

Yann LeCun has departed Meta to found the independent research startup "AMI Labs." As a leading authority in deep learning, he concluded that the current LLM-centric technical roadmap has reached its limits. Through AMI Labs, LeCun plans to focus on implementing "World Models," which allow AI to physically understand and reason about the world.

## Current Status
Yann LeCun stepped down from his role as Vice President and Chief AI Scientist at Meta to formalize the founding of AMI Labs. According to reports on January 23, 2026 (local time), a primary objective of AMI Labs is to move away from the existing LLM approach that relies on the Transformer architecture. LeCun has long argued that LLMs possess fundamental flaws in data efficiency and logical reasoning, hindering their ability to reach human-level intelligence.

The core of AMI Labs is the "World Model." This technology internalizes how the real world operates, enabling the AI to predict the consequences of specific actions in the future. Currently, the startup is building a team centered around researchers who share LeCun's vision. While details on initial investment scale and partnership structures have not been disclosed, LeCun's reputation and vision have drawn significant attention from Silicon Valley investors.

## Analysis
The emergence of AMI Labs signifies a paradigm shift in AI research. For the past several years, the industry has focused on the "Scaling Laws," injecting large amounts of data and computing resources to increase model size. However, LeCun counters this based on how humans learn. Humans understand gravity and use tools through minimal experience without reading trillions of words. AMI Labs aims to replicate this efficient learning method digitally.

This approach suggests a differentiated competition with existing tech giants. While companies like OpenAI and Google focus on improving performance by combining text and multimodal data, LeCun is focusing on the fundamental research of "understanding world structures through unsupervised learning." If AMI Labs introduces a model that understands physical causality, it could reduce hallucinations‚Äîa chronic issue in AI. However, some critics note that world models are experimental, given their high implementation difficulty and the need for non-traditional datasets.

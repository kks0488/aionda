## TL;DR
- Copilot Star integrates Matter, Tuya, AWS IoT, and older devices into one software framework.
- This system aims to create cross-manufacturer interoperability through an artificial intelligence control layer.
- Partnership discussions are in early stages as of January 27, 2026, without major manufacturer confirmation.

## Current Status
Revealed at CES 2026, Copilot Star addresses app fragmentation in the smart home market. This platform aims to be a universal framework for various communication standards. It includes technology to integrate Matter, Tuya, and AWS IoT devices into one interface. The system also intends to support devices released before industry standardization.

Copilot.cx designed the system for a consistent experience across different device types. As of January 27, 2026, the developer is seeking partnerships with various manufacturers. Specific model names or hardware specifications for compatible appliances are currently unavailable.

Observers expect the framework to operate on top of existing smart home hubs. Participation from major companies like Samsung, LG, or Sony remains unconfirmed. It is unclear if brands with existing platforms will adopt Copilot Star.

## Analysis
The smart home industry faces management complexity despite the Matter standard. Matter standardized connection pathways but did not fully integrate the user experience. Copilot Star proposes an AI layer to control devices regardless of their connection standard.

This approach could benefit small and medium-sized manufacturers. Companies without independent ecosystems can use this framework for smart home features. Large tech companies might eventually need to open their closed ecosystems.

Several concerns exist regarding this new framework. Manufacturers should adjust their data sovereignty to achieve single-app integration. Security and privacy policies require attention when user data moves to a third-party platform. The platform risks low adoption if major manufacturers do not participate.

## Practical Application
Implementation will take time as the framework is still in the partnership phase. Users and developers can monitor the integration direction presented by this platform.

**Checklist for Today:**
- Check if your smart devices use Tuya or AWS IoT platforms.
- Follow news regarding major appliance manufacturers joining the Copilot Star partnership.
- Prioritize buying products that do not depend on a specific platform.

## FAQ
**Q: Is Copilot Star a new standard replacing Matter?**
A: No, Matter is a hardware protocol. Copilot Star is a software framework for managing various standards in one place.

**Q: Can I use Samsung or LG appliances directly in the Copilot Star app?**
A: This is not possible as of January 27, 2026. These companies have not confirmed their official participation yet. Users should continue using the dedicated apps for these brands.

**Q: Does it support legacy devices?**
A: The framework aims to support older devices. Integration depends on manufacturers allowing their systems to work with Copilot Star.

## Conclusion
Copilot Star proposes an integrated control layer to solve smart home fragmentation. Success for this AI management approach depends on negotiations with manufacturers. Participation from global brands will indicate the direction of the smart home market in 2026. Users should plan technology adoption while monitoring partnership expansion.

### A 1.2 GW Ambition on the Texas Plains

The core of this collaboration lies in the numbers. 1.2 GW is a figure comparable to the generation capacity of a medium-sized nuclear power plant. This immense power is sufficient to simultaneously run approximately 400,000 of NVIDIA's next-generation Blackwell (GB200) GPUs. By jointly investing $1 billion in SB Energy, OpenAI and SoftBank have signaled their intent to secure power infrastructure and directly tackle energy supply issues‚Äîthe primary bottleneck in training next-generation AI.

The campus, to be established across three Texas regions‚ÄîMilam, Shackelford, and Abilene‚Äîwill account for approximately 24% of the total 5 GW power target for OpenAI's 'Stargate' project. This strategy goes beyond merely renting servers; it aims to secure supply chain independence and reduce reliance on cloud providers through vertical integration, spanning from energy production to operations.

### The Era Where Power is Intelligence: Why SoftBank?

The reason OpenAI chose SoftBank as a partner is clear. SoftBank, through SB Energy, already possesses large-scale land and grid connection rights. Considering that 'grid permitting' is the most time-consuming task in data center construction, the alliance with SoftBank is a shortcut that dramatically accelerates the pace of the Stargate project.

This move signifies a paradigm shift in the AI industry. While writing superior code was central in the past, securing a stable and massive power supply has now become the ticket to Artificial General Intelligence (AGI). A 1.2 GW infrastructure provides an environment for training models with parameters expanded tens of times compared to existing ones in shorter cycles or for training multiple large models in parallel. This is expected to serve as the practical foundation for developing computationally intensive AGI models.

However, it is not all rosy. Concerns regarding the stability of the Texas power grid (ERCOT) remain, and the impact of the massive energy consumption by a single facility on local community power supplies cannot be ignored. ### What Developers and Businesses Should Prepare

The expansion of OpenAI's infrastructure will ultimately lead to a 'decrease in inference costs' and an 'explosive improvement in model performance.' Developers must now prepare for multimodal agent environments that perform high-resolution video and real-time physical simulations simultaneously, moving beyond simple text processing.

1.  **Agentic AI Design**: As computational resources become more abundant, the cost of running AI agents that perform complex reasoning steps will decrease. Focus should shift from simple chatbots to designing workflows where AI can judge and execute autonomously.

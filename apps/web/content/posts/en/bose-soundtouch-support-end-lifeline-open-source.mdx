## TL;DR
- Bose plans to end SoundTouch software support in 2026.
- The company open-sourced the SoundTouch API and documentation as a support solution.
- This initiative aims to address short device cycles and environmental concerns.

Example: A high-end speaker in the corner of a living room suddenly stops working. The exterior remains pristine and internal parts stay functional. As communication standards change, playing music becomes difficult. Once the manufacturer ends server access, the device falls silent.

## Current Status
Bose will end software support for the SoundTouch product line in 2026. Technical limitations and the transition to newer platforms influenced this decision. Unlike many companies, Bose introduced a contingency plan. The solution is called Lifeline. 

Lifeline is an open-source bridge technology for SoundTouch hardware. It helps devices operate without official apps or cloud services. Users can connect their devices to local networks after 2026. They can also integrate them with modern services. 

Bose provides source code for the community to maintain device functionality. This approach differs from competitors who restrict older products. SoundTouch users have a technical safety net to continue using their devices.

## Analysis
This decision responds to criticisms regarding planned obsolescence. Smart speakers can have a long hardware lifespan. However, they often stop functioning when software support ends. Bose attempts to align digital and physical hardware lifespans via Lifeline. 

This project could enhance brand trust and support governance goals. The open-source approach may involve technical difficulties for average users. Complex configurations could limit effectiveness for some users. 

Some argue the manufacturer is shifting maintenance to the community. Discussions regarding manufacturer responsibility may increase as 2026 approaches.
